Just Stop Oil protesters smash glass on painting at National Gallery

Just Stop Oil protesters have been arrested after smashing the glass covering a Diego Velázquez painting at the National Gallery in London.

Two activists targeted the glass on the Rokeby Venus painting with safety hammers before they were arrested on suspicion of criminal damage.

The artwork, which was painted by Velázquez in the 1600s, was slashed by the suffragette Mary Richardson in 1914. One of those involved on Monday said: "Women did not get the vote by voting; it is time for deeds not words."

Just Stop Oil said more than 100 of its supporters had taken action to demand no new oil and gas.

The government has revealed plans to mandate annual oil and gas licensing in the North Sea in an attempt to reduce dependency on "hostile foreign regimes".

The Rokeby Venus has historical significance as a target of protest action, having been previously damaged by suffragette Mary Richardson over a century ago in her campaign for women's voting rights. The activists appeared to draw parallels between their environmental campaign and the historical suffragette movement.



The National Gallery houses one of the world's most important collections of paintings, making it a high-profile venue for protest action. The Rokeby Venus, formally known as "The Toilet of Venus," is considered one of Velázquez's masterpieces and represents one of the few surviving nude paintings by the Spanish master.

The incident highlights the ongoing tension between environmental activists and government energy policy, with Just Stop Oil continuing their campaign against new fossil fuel projects. The group's demand for no new oil and gas licensing comes as the government moves in the opposite direction with plans for annual North Sea licensing rounds.

The targeting of cultural institutions and artworks has become a recurring tactic for environmental protesters seeking to draw attention to climate issues, though such actions often generate significant controversy and debate about appropriate forms of protest.

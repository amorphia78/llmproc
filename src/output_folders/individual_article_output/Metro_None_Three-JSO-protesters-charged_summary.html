Three JSO protesters charged after soup thrown at Van Gogh painting – again

PRIMARY: Three Just Stop Oil protesters - Stephen Simpson (61), Phillipa Green (24), and Mary Somerville (77) - threw Heinz vegetable soup at two Van Gogh 'Sunflowers' paintings at the National Gallery's exhibition. They demanded accountability for fossil fuel executives and politicians, stating "Future generations will regard these prisoners of conscience to be on the right side of history." All three were charged with criminal damage.

SECONDARY: The article extensively covers the earlier jailing of Phoebe Plummer and Anna Holland for a similar soup attack on the same painting in 2022, receiving sentences totaling three years and eight months. Previous protests included activists gluing themselves to Constable's The Hay Wain.



OTHER FOCAL: The court proceedings revealed concerns about potential damage to the £28,000 frame, with £10,000 estimated damage from the previous attack.

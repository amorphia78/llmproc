ECO WAR We were shot at like in a James Bond movie, says Greenpeace activist in new BBC documentary about oil rig mission

THE new documentary investigates the British Greenpeace activists who planned to protest from the side of an oil rig in the Arctic.

PRIMARY: In 2013, 30 Greenpeace environmentalists including five Brits targeted Russian oil company Gazprom's drilling operation in the Barents Sea. Their plan was to attach a protest pod to the oil rig to halt production and draw attention to Arctic fossil fuel extraction. Instead, they were shot at by Russian coastguard, arrested by armed counter-terrorism soldiers on piracy charges, and jailed for two months facing 15 years in prison.

SECONDARY: The activists managed to smuggle footage out of prison in a matchbox, and Paul McCartney wrote a letter to Putin supporting their release, which helped generate international pressure.



OTHER FOCAL: The article focuses on a new BBC documentary series about the incident, featuring unseen footage and testimony from the protesters known as the "Arctic 30."

TONE: The article presents the protest sympathetically, portraying the activists as brave environmental defenders facing brutal overreaction from Russian authorities.

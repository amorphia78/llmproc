Charges against 34 people dropped over plastic pollution protest at Unilever HQ

PRIMARY: Thirty-four Greenpeace activists had criminal charges dropped for blockading Unilever's London headquarters in September, protesting the company's alleged failure to tackle plastic pollution. Eight faced aggravated trespass charges and 26 faced "locking on" charges under new legislation. Activists climbed the building, attached artwork, blocked entrances, and locked themselves to large Dove product models.

SECONDARY: The protest was part of Greenpeace's ongoing campaign against Unilever after the company rolled back plastic reduction targets.



OTHER FOCAL: The article discusses broader concerns about new protest laws restricting peaceful demonstration rights and their "chilling effect on democracy."

Third arrest after Stonehenge sprayed with powder paint

PRIMARY: Just Stop Oil campaigners sprayed orange powder paint on the historic Stonehenge site near Salisbury on June 19th. Three people have been arrested - a man in his 70s, a woman in her 20s, and a man in his 30s from Essex. The protesters demanded the next UK government end extraction and burning of oil, gas and coal by 2030.



SECONDARY: No secondary protests mentioned.

OTHER FOCAL: English Heritage quickly removed the orange powder with no visible damage to the stones, though the removal process could potentially erode the fragile stone and damage lichens.

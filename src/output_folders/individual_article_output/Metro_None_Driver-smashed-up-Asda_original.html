Driver smashed up Asda lorry after suffering road rage from oil protesters

An engineer has narrowly avoided jail after attacking an Asda lorry with a crowbar.

Sidney Smith, 25, caused £8,000 worth of damage to the truck when he became enraged while sat in traffic caused by Just Stop Oil protesters.

He was on his way home to Brentwood, Essex, after a 12-hour night shift when he undertook the lorry and clipped it on October 18, 2022.

He got out of his car and started shouting at the Asda driver before ripping off the number plate and windscreen wiper.

He then got back into his car and started braking sharply in front of it until he caused a collision.

Prosecutor Richard Lamb said: ‘He went to the boot of his vehicle and got a red bar out and started striking the driver’s door with it, he did that four times and was shouting and was angry.

‘The lorry driver thought the window would break and he was shouting and pacing about with the bar. He then got in his vehicle again and sped off.’

Smith admitted at Maidstone Magistrates’ Court to losing it but claimed the lorry driver had clipped his car, not the other way round.

He was given a three-month prison sentence suspended for 18 months and banned from driving for 18 months.

He previously served a suspended prison sentence for dangerous driving in 2016.

Andrew Thompson, defending, said his client was ‘ashamed’ and wrote a letter of apology.

His defence said if Smith was jailed for the new offence he would lose his job and his home, as he owns his house and business.

Mr Thompson added: ‘He deeply regrets what happened, he’s embarrassed and if possible would like to meet the Asda driver and shake his hand and apologise.’

As well as the ban and suspended sentence, he was ordered to pay £500 to Asda and ordered to carry out 100 hours of community service.

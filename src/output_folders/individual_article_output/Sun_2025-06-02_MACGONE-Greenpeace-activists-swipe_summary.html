MAC-GONE Greenpeace activists swipe Emmanuel Macron waxwork from Paris’s Grevin Museum before using it in anti-Russia protest

The figure was placed in front of the Russian embassy, as activists urged France to halt gas and fertiliser imports from Moscow

PRIMARY: Three Greenpeace activists (two women and one man) stole a £35,000 waxwork of French President Emmanuel Macron from Paris's Grevin Museum by disguising themselves as staff. They then placed the figure in front of the Russian embassy, demanding France halt gas and fertiliser imports from Moscow, criticizing Macron for supporting Ukraine while allowing French firms to continue trading with Russia.

SECONDARY: No secondary protests mentioned.



OTHER FOCAL: The article discusses the poor quality of the waxwork, which has been widely ridiculed and compared to Thunderbird puppets, with even museum staff admitting it doesn't resemble Macron well.

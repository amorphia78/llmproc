Just Stop Oil soup throwers ‘are daring iconoclasts, not criminals’

Art experts call for activists who attacked Van Gogh’s Sunflowers to be spared jail

PRIMARY: Two Just Stop Oil activists, Phoebe Plummer and Anna Holland (both 22), threw soup on Van Gogh's Sunflowers at London's National Gallery in October 2022. The painting wasn't damaged but the frame was. They were found guilty of criminal damage and face potential jail sentences at Friday's sentencing.

SECONDARY: The article mentions Liberate Tate's past performance interventions protesting BP sponsorship of Tate galleries starting in 2010, which successfully ended oil company sponsorship.



OTHER FOCAL: Over 100 art experts, artists, and academics are campaigning to spare the activists from jail, arguing their act represents artistic iconoclasm similar to works by Banksy and other recognized artists.

TONE: The article presents a positive tone toward the protest, framing it through the lens of art experts who view it as legitimate artistic expression rather than criminal activity.

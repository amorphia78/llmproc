Greta Thunberg was given ‘final warning’ before London arrest

Activist says ‘history’s judgment will not be gentle’ for those behind climate crisis after day in court on public order charges

PRIMARY: Greta Thunberg and four other activists (two from Fossil Free London and two from Greenpeace) were arrested outside London's InterContinental Hotel during a climate demonstration targeting oil executives meeting inside for the Energy Intelligence Forum. After police imposed conditions requiring protesters to move to a designated area, Thunberg refused to comply despite warnings and was arrested. Some protesters had abseiled down the hotel building.

SECONDARY: Environmental protesters demonstrated outside Westminster Magistrates' Court "in solidarity" with the defendants, holding banners reading "Climate protest is not a crime."



OTHER FOCAL: The article focuses on the court proceedings where all five defendants face charges under the Public Order Act for breaching police conditions during the protest.

TONE: The article maintains a neutral, factual tone while giving Thunberg space to present her perspective on climate activism.

Over 100 flights cancelled after protesters glue themselves to runway at Frankfurt Airport

The airport warned of further delays to passengers and more potential cancellations. Eight people have reportedly been taken into police custody over the demonstration.

PRIMARY: Six climate protesters from Last Generation glued themselves to the runway at Frankfurt Airport after cutting through the perimeter fence, wearing orange vests and carrying "Oil kills" signs. This disrupted around 140 flights, with eight people arrested. The group demands governments end fossil fuel extraction and burning by 2030.

SECONDARY: Five Last Generation protesters disrupted Cologne-Bonn Airport earlier this week, halting flights for three hours. Similar protests occurred across Europe including at Heathrow, with coordinated actions in Finland, Norway, Spain and Switzerland.



OTHER FOCAL: Germany recently approved legislation imposing harsher penalties on airport trespassers, potentially including two-year prison sentences, as officials call the protests "dangerous, dumb and criminal."

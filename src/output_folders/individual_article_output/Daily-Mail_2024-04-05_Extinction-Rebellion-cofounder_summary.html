Extinction Rebellion co-founder Roger Hallam is spared jail over plot to close Heathrow by flying drones near the airport in bid to force the Government to abandon plans for third runway

Roger Hallam and other eco-activists launched the protest in September 2019.

PRIMARY: Roger Hallam (Extinction Rebellion co-founder), Larch Maxey, and Michael Lynch-White plotted to close Heathrow Airport by flying drones near it in September 2019 under the banner "Heathrow Pause." Their demand was to force the Government to abandon plans for a third runway at Heathrow. The protest failed to attract hundreds as planned and caused no actual flight disruptions, though policing costs exceeded £1 million. All three received suspended prison sentences.

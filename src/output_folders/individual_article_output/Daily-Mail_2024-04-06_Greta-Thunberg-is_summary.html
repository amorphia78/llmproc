Greta Thunberg is detained twice by Dutch police during protest over fossil fuel in The Hague - as a video shows the moment two burly cops dump the Swedish climate activist on a coach

Thunberg and a group of marchers blocked a main road on Saturday.

PRIMARY: Greta Thunberg and hundreds of protesters blocked a main road and traffic intersection in The Hague, organized by Extinction Rebellion. They carried placards demanding "Stop fuel subsidies now!" and protested the government's delay in implementing fossil fuel subsidy cuts until 2030-2035. Thunberg was arrested twice and briefly detained before being released without charges.

SECONDARY: The protest was part of a broader European campaign involving 30 movements from 15 countries, with simultaneous actions in 10 European countries and planned future blockades including May 4 actions.



OTHER FOCAL: The article focuses on the Dutch government's slow implementation of fossil fuel subsidy cuts despite parliamentary and popular support, and the $7 trillion global fossil fuel subsidies according to IMF.

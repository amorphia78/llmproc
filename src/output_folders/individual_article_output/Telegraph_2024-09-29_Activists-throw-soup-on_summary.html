Activists throw soup on British embassy in Berlin over Just Stop Oil sentences

Three women from direct-action group Last Generation stage protest in show of ‘solidarity’ with Van Gogh vandalisers

PRIMARY: Three women from Last Generation threw tomato soup over the walls of the British embassy in Berlin, protesting prison sentences given to Just Stop Oil activists who vandalized Van Gogh's Sunflowers. The protesters held signs featuring photos of the sentenced activists, calling them "climate prisoners" and accusing British authorities of "endangering democracy."

SECONDARY: Similar protests occurred at British missions in Amsterdam, Paris and Rome. Last Generation has previously disrupted roads and airports by gluing themselves to surfaces, switched off oil pipelines, and spray-painted Brandenburg Gate orange.



OTHER FOCAL: Munich prosecutors are investigating whether Last Generation should be classified as a "criminal organisation," and the German government is considering harsher penalties for airport tarmac trespassing.

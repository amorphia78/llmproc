Mum got four years for renting a safehouse for Just Stop Oil

The family of Louise Lancaster, who was sentenced this month for her role in the M25 protests, say they are shocked at the punishment but back her fight against climate change

PRIMARY: Louise Lancaster, a 58-year-old former special educational needs coordinator, was sentenced to four years in prison for her role in organizing M25 protests in November 2022. She rented an Airbnb safehouse for protesters and bought climbing equipment for the action that recruited 45 volunteers to climb gantries over the M25, causing massive disruption to 700,000 vehicles over four days. The protest aimed to force the government to end new oil and gas exploration in the North Sea.



SECONDARY: Lancaster had previously climbed an M25 gantry herself in an earlier July 2022 protest.

OTHER FOCAL: The article focuses heavily on her family's reaction to the sentence, with her ex-husband and three children expressing shock at the harsh punishment while supporting her climate activism.

TONE: The article presents a sympathetic portrayal of Lancaster and her family, questioning the severity of the sentence.

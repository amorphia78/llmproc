Third person arrested after Stonehenge sprayed with orange paint powder

A man in his 30s has been detained, following the arrest of two other people after the incident more than a week ago.

PRIMARY: Two Just Stop Oil protesters wearing white shirts ran up to Stonehenge and sprayed orange paint powder on several stones of the prehistoric monument before sitting cross-legged on grass. The protesters demanded the next UK government agree a plan with other countries to end extraction and burning of fossil fuels by 2030. Members of the public tried to stop them by dragging them away.



SECONDARY: No secondary protest actions mentioned.

OTHER FOCAL: Three people have now been arrested in connection with the incident. English Heritage quickly removed the powder but warned the removal process could damage fragile stones and rare lichens.

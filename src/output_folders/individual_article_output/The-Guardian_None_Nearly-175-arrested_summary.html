Nearly 175 arrested as climate protesters target France’s TotalEnergies and key investor

PRIMARY: Climate activists, including several hundred Extinction Rebellion members, targeted the Paris headquarters of Amundi (a major TotalEnergies shareholder) during its annual meeting. A few dozen protesters forced their way into Amundi's tower block, daubing graffiti on walls and smashing windows, injuring eight security staff. Police detained 173 people. The activists demand action against TotalEnergies' oil and gas activities, which they say contribute to global warming and biodiversity destruction.



SECONDARY: Greenpeace members separately protested at TotalEnergies' annual meeting, unfurling a "Wanted" banner calling the CEO "the leader of France's most polluting company" before police removed it.

OTHER FOCAL: TotalEnergies' CEO defended developing new oilfields to meet global demand and shareholders approved the company's climate strategy and renewed his leadership.

TONE: The article maintains a neutral, factual tone toward the protests.

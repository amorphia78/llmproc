First trial of Just Stop Oil activists under new anti-protest laws begins

PRIMARY: Three Just Stop Oil supporters - Phoebe Plummer, Chiara Sarti and Daniel Hall - are on trial for conducting a slow march that blocked traffic on Cromwell Road in west London for less than 20 minutes in November. They are charged under the new Public Order Act 2023 for "interference with key national infrastructure" and demand the government halt all new oil, gas and coal licences. Two protesters were held on remand for over two weeks.



SECONDARY: No secondary protests mentioned.

OTHER FOCAL: The trial represents a major test case for new anti-protest laws that critics say give police excessive discretion to prosecute protesters, with potential 12-month jail sentences for disrupting key infrastructure.

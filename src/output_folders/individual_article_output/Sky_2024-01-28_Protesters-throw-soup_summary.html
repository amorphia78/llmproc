Protesters throw soup at Mona Lisa painting in Paris

Two climate activists threw red soup at the Mona Lisa in Paris. Footage shows two women ducking a security barrier to get close to the painting, shouting "our farming system is sick".

PRIMARY: Two climate activists from Riposte Alimentaire threw red soup at the Mona Lisa in the Louvre, Paris. The women, aged 24 and 63, crossed security barriers while shouting "our farming system is sick" and demanding "sustainable food social security." Both were arrested, though the painting was protected by glass.

SECONDARY: French farmers have been using tractors to create blockades and slow traffic across the country, protesting low wages, rising costs, and regulations, with some threatening to converge on Paris.



OTHER FOCAL: The article mentions previous attacks on the Mona Lisa and other artworks by environmental activists, including cake-smearing and soup-throwing incidents at galleries in London and Madrid.

Arrests after gas protesters scale chimney stacks

PRIMARY: Eight Greenpeace activists climbed two 75-meter high chimney stacks at Staythorpe Power Station in Nottinghamshire to protest gas's role in setting electricity prices. The protesters targeted the RWE-owned gas-fired power station, with four coming down before midday and the others after 16:30 BST. All eight were arrested for aggravated trespassing and remain in custody.

SECONDARY: No secondary protest actions mentioned.



OTHER FOCAL: Police emphasized preventing future security breaches, while RWE stated the power station remained operational throughout the incident and prioritized everyone's safety on site.

Stonehenge covered in powder paint by Just Stop Oil

PRIMARY: Two Just Stop Oil protesters sprayed orange powder paint on Stonehenge's ancient stones, targeting the 5,000-year-old UNESCO World Heritage site. The protesters were identified as Niamh Lynch, a 21-year-old student from Oxford, and Rajan Naidu, a 73-year-old from Birmingham. They demanded the next UK government end extraction and burning of fossil fuels by 2030. Both were arrested on suspicion of damaging the ancient monument.

SECONDARY: No secondary protest actions mentioned.



OTHER FOCAL: The incident occurred the day before Summer Solstice celebrations. Political leaders including PM Rishi Sunak and Labour's Keir Starmer condemned the action as vandalism. Archaeologists expressed concern about potential damage to prehistoric markings and rare lichen on the stones.

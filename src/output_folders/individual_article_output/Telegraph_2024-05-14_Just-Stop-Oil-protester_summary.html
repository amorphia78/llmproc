Just Stop Oil protester who painted Scotland Yard sign orange cleared after comparison to Rosa Parks

Lora Johnson sprayed the sign with paint in Oct 2022 amid a series of coordinated climate demonstrations by environmental campaigners

PRIMARY: Lora Johnson, a 39-year-old Just Stop Oil protester from Suffolk, used a paint-filled fire extinguisher to spray Scotland Yard's revolving sign with orange paint in October 2022. Her action was part of coordinated climate demonstrations protesting the government's approval of new oil and gas licenses. She was cleared of criminal damage charges after a six-day trial.

SECONDARY: Other demonstrators blocked the road in front of the Metropolitan Police headquarters along Victoria Embankment as part of the coordinated actions.



OTHER FOCAL: Johnson's barrister compared her actions to historical civil rights figures like Rosa Parks and the Suffragettes, arguing that direct action is part of democracy.

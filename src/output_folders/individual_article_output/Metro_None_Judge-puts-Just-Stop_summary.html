Judge puts Just Stop Oil protester in her place over 9,000-mile trip to India

PRIMARY: Five Just Stop Oil protesters (Hannah Taylor, 23; Lydia Gribbin, 28; Hanan Ameur, 22; Noah Crane, 18; and Poppy Bliss, 19) stormed the stage during a West End performance of Les Miserables at London's Sondheim Theatre. They unfurled orange Just Stop Oil flags and chained themselves to the stage with bicycle locks, forcing the show to halt and costing the theatre £80,000. The action aimed to raise climate crisis awareness and stop fossil fuel use.

SECONDARY: Just Stop Oil also covered a dinosaur skeleton at the Natural History Museum in orange powder as part of their climate activism campaign.



OTHER FOCAL: The court proceedings focus on the judge refusing to delay the trial when one defendant requested postponement for a trip to India, highlighting potential financial liability for the protesters.

TONE: The article takes a negative tone toward the protest, emphasizing disruption costs, audience anger, and the judge's dismissive response to the defendant's travel request.

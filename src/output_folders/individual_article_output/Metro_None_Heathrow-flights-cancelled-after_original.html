If you’ve ever flown from London, it is likely you’ve gone through one of the terminals at Heathrow.

Now the airport has revealed how many passengers it had so far this year – and it is more than the population of Canada.

The transport hub says 39.8 million passengers travelled through the gates in the first six months of this year.

In the same period last year, it had 37.1 million passengers, meaning numbers are up by 7.3%.

In 2019 – before the Coronavirus pandemic – it had 38.8 million passengers.

Heathrow’s chief executive Thomas Woldbye told PA news agency that serving the new figure was ‘no easy feat.’

A total of 765 tonnes of cargo also flew through Heathrow in the first half of 2024, he said.

What about the controversial third runway?

The airport boss said it is ‘reviewing’ the project to build a third runway, which got parliamentary approval in June 2018.

But the project has been marred by legal challenges over its environmental impact and the Covid pandemic.

Flights at Heathrow Airport were nearly disrupted this morning after Just Stop Oil protesters tried to get in near the security fence.

Nine protesters have been arrested, the Met said.

Now UK’s busiest airport has commented on the incident, which the climate group said was to protest against the ‘irresponsible’ use of fossil fuels.

A Heathrow spokesperson said: ‘Thanks to swift action from the police and airport colleagues, there is no disruption to passenger journeys.

‘Heathrow continues to operate as normal today. We are in full agreement that the aviation industry needs to decarbonise, but unlawful and irresponsible protest activity is not the way forward and will not be tolerated.’

Just Stop Oil protesters were arrested right outside Heathrow airport’s security fence this morning.

The climate group’s planned action was foiled at the doorstep of the busy transport hub by police officers.

Nine protesters were arrested, the Met confirmed on social media.

The force said: ‘Nine Just Stop Oil activists have been arrested this morning for conspiring to disrupt Heathrow Airport.

‘Thanks to the work of officers involved, those arrested have been taken into custody and the Met has prevented significant disruption to the airport and travelling public.’

It is thought the incident did not cause any disruption to flights in and out of Heathrow.

Just Stop Oil said that seven of its supporters were arrested at around 9am, before a further two were taken into custody on suspicion of conspiracy to disrupt Heathrow.

Elsewhere, the group’s supporters blocked air traffic at Cologne Bonn Airport, causing international delays, Just Stop Oil said.

Extinction Rebellion protesters also blocked security gates at Helsinki Vantaa Airport in Finland, while five protesters made it to the taxiway at Barcelona airport.

A spokesperson for the group said: ‘We refuse to die for fossil fuels. Continued burning of oil, gas and coal, as we pass irreversible tipping points that threaten to spin our climate out of control in a rapidly accelerating way, is jeopardising the stability on which our entire society depends.’

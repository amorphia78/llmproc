Three arrests after Just Stop Oil protesters spray orange paint on London's Wellington Arch

The latest protest comes just weeks after the government backed the development of the UK's largest untapped oil and gas field - and follows similar action by other campaigners.

PRIMARY: Three Just Stop Oil protesters sprayed orange paint on Wellington Arch in London using fire extinguishers and set off smoke flares at the Grade I-listed monument. The activists included Joshua Lane, 26, an engineer from Sheffield, and Joe Hogan, 40, from Hertfordshire. They were protesting against new fossil fuel licences, specifically following government backing of the UK's largest untapped oil and gas field at Rosebank.

SECONDARY: Three climate campaigners received community orders for invading the pitch at Lord's cricket ground, and 12 Just Stop Oil supporters face court proceedings for M25 protests, including 76-year-old grandmother Gaie Delap who climbed a motorway gantry.

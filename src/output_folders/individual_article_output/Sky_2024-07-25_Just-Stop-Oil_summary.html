Just Stop Oil activists found guilty after throwing soup over Van Gogh's Sunflowers

A video of the demonstration in 2022 showed the two women wearing Just Stop Oil T-shirts and glueing one hand each to the wall below the painting, which is protected by a pane of glass.

PRIMARY: Two Just Stop Oil activists, Phoebe Plummer and Anna Holland (both 22), were found guilty of criminal damage after throwing Heinz tomato soup over Van Gogh's Sunflowers at London's National Gallery in October 2022. They also glued their hands to the wall below the £72.5m painting, which was protected by glass but suffered minor frame damage. Their protest aimed to stop the government issuing new oil and gas licences.



SECONDARY: Two activists had previously glued themselves to Constable's The Hay Wain at the same gallery in July 2022.

OTHER FOCAL: The article mentions Judge Christopher Hehir previously jailed Just Stop Oil co-founder Roger Hallam for five years, and the activists face sentencing on 27 September.

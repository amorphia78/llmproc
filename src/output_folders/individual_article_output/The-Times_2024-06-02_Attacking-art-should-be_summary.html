Attacking art ‘should be criminal offence even if there’s no damage’

French culture minister demands law to prosecute activists who target paintings that are behind protective glass

PRIMARY: An environmental activist from Riposte Alimentaire stuck a poster on the glass case protecting Claude Monet's Coquelicots at Paris's Orsay Museum. The group campaigns for a "social security system of sustainable food." The 1873 painting was undamaged and returned to display the same day.

SECONDARY: The same group previously threw soup on the Mona Lisa's glass case at the Louvre in January. Just Stop Oil campaigners glued themselves to Vermeer's Girl with the Pearl Earring in the Netherlands in 2022.



OTHER FOCAL: French culture minister Rachida Dati demands legal reform to prosecute activists targeting glass-protected artworks, even without damage, calling for criminal policy against this "new form of crime."

TONE: The article presents a negative tone toward the protests, using terms like "iconoclasts" and framing the actions as criminal attacks on culture.

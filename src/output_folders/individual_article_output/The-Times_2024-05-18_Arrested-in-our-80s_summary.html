Arrested in our 80s over Magna Carta: meet the Just Stop Oil pensioners

PRIMARY: Two elderly women, Rev Dr Sue Parfitt (82) and retired biology teacher Judy Bruce (85), were arrested after chipping the display case of Magna Carta at the British Library with a hammer and chisel while holding a banner reading "The government is breaking the law." The Just Stop Oil action resulted in £2,000 in criminal damage charges. The protesters demand government action on climate change and want to stop North Sea drilling.

SECONDARY: The pair previously unfurled a banner during a thanksgiving service at St Paul's Cathedral reading "Will our children thank us?" and met while "sitting in front of an oil tanker."



OTHER FOCAL: The article explores how these respectable elderly women became radical climate activists later in life, their Christian motivations, and public criticism of Just Stop Oil's disruptive tactics.

TONE: The article takes a sympathetic, humanizing tone toward the protesters, portraying them as principled individuals driven by genuine concern for humanity's future.

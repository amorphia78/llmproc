UK climate activists convicted in first trial of new anti-protest laws

PRIMARY: Three Just Stop Oil activists - Phoebe Plummer, Chiara Sarti and Daniel Hall - were convicted for marching slowly along Earls Court Road for 20 minutes, causing traffic delays for several hours. This was the first jury trial under new anti-protest laws banning interference with key national infrastructure. The protesters demanded the government ban new oil and gas licences in the UK.

SECONDARY: No secondary protests mentioned.



OTHER FOCAL: The trial tested new section 7 of the Public Order Act 2023, which critics call repressive. The judge restricted defendants from presenting climate crisis evidence or justification defenses, repeatedly interrupting their closing speeches.

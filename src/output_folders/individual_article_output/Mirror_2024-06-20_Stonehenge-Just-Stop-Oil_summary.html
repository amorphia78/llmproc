Stonehenge Just Stop Oil paint-sprayers named as Oxford student, 21, and Quaker, 73

PRIMARY: Two Just Stop Oil protesters sprayed orange corn flour and paint on Stonehenge stones, targeting the ancient monument. The protesters were identified as Rajan Naidu, 73, a Quaker from Birmingham, and Niamh Lynch, 21, an Oxford student and youth representative at the British Trust for Ornithology. They demand the government sign a legally binding treaty to phase out fossil fuels by 2030.

SECONDARY: Just Stop Oil threatened that supporters from multiple countries including Austria, Canada, Norway, Netherlands and Switzerland will join resistance actions this summer if governments don't take meaningful climate action.



OTHER FOCAL: English Heritage confirmed no visible damage occurred and the site reopened for the solstice after cleaning. Police released both protesters on bail pending further investigation.

TONE: The article presents a negative tone toward the protest, describing it as "vandalism" and noting it caused "fury across the UK."

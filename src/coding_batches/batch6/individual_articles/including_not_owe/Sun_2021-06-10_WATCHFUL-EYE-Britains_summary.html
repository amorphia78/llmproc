WATCHFUL EYE Britain’s newest £3.3bn aircraft carrier keeps watch in Carbis Bay ahead of Biden’s showdown with Boris Johnson

Britain's new £3.3billion aircraft carrier, HMS Prince of Wales, is keeping watch over Carbis Bay as the three-day G7 summit commences. The vessel forms part of a secure 'ring of steel' ahead of the meeting between President Joe Biden and Boris Johnson.

The security measures include HMS Northumberland, a Type 23 frigate, positioned off the coast to help identify potential threats from air and sea. A Giraffe agile multi-beam radar system has been deployed for coastal surveillance, and 15 explosives search dogs and handlers from the army are operating under police command.

The airspace above Cornwall is largely shut, with police reserving the option to deploy one of their 18 drones if necessary. Around 1,000 police will be staying on a cruise liner at Falmouth harbour and in camper vans parked at the RAF base in Newquay.

The cost of policing the summit has been put at £70million, with officers called in from other forces to support Devon and Cornwall Police. As part of the security ramp-up, police have closed down up to 30 bridges across the A30, one of the main roads leading into and through Cornwall.



A barrage of steel fences have been planted around Tregenna Castle, where President Biden will be staying, with many roads and footpaths remaining shut for up to 21 days. Residents living near the meeting venue will have to show two forms of ID to be allowed past checkpoints.

Devon and Cornwall Police are expecting protests over the weekend from groups like Extinction Rebellion. More than 6,500 police from forces across the UK are set to be deployed during the summit.

Leaders from Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, the UK and the US are meeting in Cornwall for the annual G7 summit this Friday and are expected to discuss Covid vaccine donations to developing countries and a 15 percent global tax rate.

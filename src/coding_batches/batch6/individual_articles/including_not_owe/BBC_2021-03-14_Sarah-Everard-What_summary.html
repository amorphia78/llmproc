Sarah Everard: What went wrong at the Clapham vigil?

The vigil for Sarah Everard on Clapham Common in south London turned into a PR disaster for the Metropolitan Police when their attempts to break up the gathering led to confrontations with attendees. The event, which was not officially sanctioned due to COVID-19 restrictions, saw hundreds of people gather at the bandstand around 18:00 GMT.

Assistant Commissioner Helen Ball stated that officers faced a difficult decision as the crowd posed a "very real risk of easily transmitting Covid-19." The police attempted to disperse the gathering and remove individuals they believed were contributing to the crowding.

The police's handling of the vigil has drawn criticism, with some accusing them of oppressing women. Questions have been raised about why this demonstration was considered a greater Covid risk than others, such as the Black Lives Matter protests last summer.

Home Secretary Priti Patel has requested an external review of the event by Her Majesty's Inspectorate of Constabulary. The outcome of this review may determine the future of Metropolitan Police Commissioner Dame Cressida Dick, who is facing calls to resign.

The incident highlights the challenges of public order policing, particularly during the pandemic, and the ongoing debate about the balance between public health measures and the right to protest.

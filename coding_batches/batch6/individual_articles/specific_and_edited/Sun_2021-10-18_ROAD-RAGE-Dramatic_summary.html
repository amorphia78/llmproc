ROAD RAGE Dramatic moment furious motorist in Range Rover drives into Insulate Britain protesters blocking the road

Footage captures a dramatic confrontation between a motorist and Insulate Britain protesters blocking the road. The incident occurred during a school run near the Dartford Crossing at Purfleet, Essex, where protesters had disrupted traffic at an industrial estate.

A woman in a Range Rover, attempting to take her 11-year-old son to school, confronts the protesters. She screams at them, "Let me take my son to school," and demands they move out of the way. After her initial pleas are ignored, the woman returns to her vehicle and drives at a pair of protesters, pushing them along the road.

The confrontation escalates as the woman exits her car again, shouting, "I don't care what the f***ing issue is, my son needs to get to school." Other bystanders can be heard swearing at the protesters and encouraging the woman's actions.



Insulate Britain, an offshoot of Extinction Rebellion, has been causing disruptions for motorists in recent weeks with sit-ins on major roads, including the M25. The group is demanding that the UK Government implement policy and funding for a national home insulation programme, starting with social housing. They are calling on Prime Minister Boris Johnson to make "significant legislative change to start lowering emissions."

The protesters have caused misery for motorists in recent weeks with sit-ins on the M25, Britain's busiest motorway. Their actions have faced accusations of hypocrisy, with the group's ringleader Liam Norton admitting he "doesn't care" about insulation and reportedly not insulating his own home.

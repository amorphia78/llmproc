London Marathon 2023: Everything you need to know about this year's race

The London Marathon is set to take place this Sunday, with more than 45,000 runners expected to tackle the 26.2-mile course. The event returns to its traditional spring date for the first time since the Covid-19 pandemic, with live coverage beginning on BBC One, BBC iPlayer and online from 08:30 BST.

The men's and women's elite races feature some of the fastest runners in history. Great Britain's Mo Farah will be running his last marathon, describing it as an "emotional" day as he begins to think about retirement. The women's field includes marathon record holder Brigid Kosgei and reigning Olympic champion Peres Jepchirchir.

The wheelchair events see the return of course record holders and defending champions Marcel Hug and Catherine Debrunner, with a record total prize pot of $253,500 (Â£204,138) on offer.



Behind the elite runners, thousands of inspiring individuals will participate, raising millions for charity. This year's official race charity is Great Ormond Street Hospital. Among the famous faces taking part is Radio 1 presenter Adele Roberts, who is targeting a world record less than a year after being given the all-clear from bowel cancer.

For the first time, a non-binary gender option is featured in the mass participation element of the race. Many runners have had to carry out their training while fasting for Ramadan, including Olympic champion Sifan Hassan.

The weather forecast for Sunday is mixed, with cloud, isolated showers, and a gentle breeze predicted for the morning, turning dry with sunny spells later on.

That's the way to deal with them! Dramatic moment security guards wrestle two Just Stop Oil activists to the floor after they leapt over barricades in failed bid to stage stunt at Dippy the dinosaur exhibit in Coventry

It is understood activists were intending to spray the dinosaur exhibit with paint. But security guards hauled them to the ground before they could reach it.

Two Just Stop Oil eco-zealots were tackled by security as they attempted to enter a dinosaur exhibit at a museum. The protesters, Daniel Knorr, 21, and Victoria Lindsell, 67, were thwarted as they tried to enter the 'Dippy the Dinosaur' display at the Herbert Art Gallery & Museum in Coventry.

Footage shows the pair climbing over a low metal barrier before being tackled to the ground by staff in high-vis jackets. It is understood they were intending to spray paint the dinosaur exhibit before being stopped by security. One staff member seized Mr Knorr's rucksack, while another tackled Ms Lindsell and shouted: 'Stop it, stop it now. Do you understand?'

The demonstrators removed jumpers to reveal white Just Stop Oil t-shirts before they were removed. They were subsequently led away in handcuffs by West Midlands Police officers.

Daniel Knorr is a student from Oxford, while Victoria Lindsell is a millionaire English language teacher from Leamington, Warwickshire. Both expressed feeling they had 'no choice' but to take part in the stunt, citing concerns about climate change and government inaction.



A spokesman for West Midlands Police confirmed two people were arrested in the museum at around 10am on suspicion of conspiracy to cause criminal damage. The force added that 'two large bags of dry paint were also seized by officers'.

Just Stop Oil demanded that the government stop all new UK fossil fuel projects and called on employees and directors of UK cultural institutions to join in civil resistance against the government's policies.

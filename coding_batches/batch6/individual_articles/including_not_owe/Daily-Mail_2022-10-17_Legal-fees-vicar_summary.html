Legal fees, vicar? Reverend, 63, who was jailed for Just Stop Oil protest outside fuel terminal - breaking a court injunction - launches fundraiser to pay £2,000 bill

Eco-warrior vicar Mark Coleman, 63, is asking the public to help pay his legal bills after joining Just Stop Oil protests. The retired clergyman, who suffers from Parkinson's disease, is pleading for assistance to cover his legal and travel costs, which have put him £2,000 out of pocket.

Coleman was one of 56 Just Stop Oil protestors who blocked the Kingsbury oil terminal in Kingsbury, Warwickshire, in August. Following the protest, he spent a week in prison on remand for contempt of court. The vicar has been arrested four times this year while campaigning with the group.

In a GoFundMe appeal page, Coleman said he needs help with the "financial burden" because he doesn't "have a future." He is trying to raise £1,000 and has already had £735 donated to him by 22 people in just three days.

Coleman stated, "I am committed to protecting people from disaster from a heating climate, particularly with the UK government's plans for new oil and gas projects. These plans will push us further down the road to destruction."



The retired vicar of St Chad's and St Mary's churches in Rochdale, Greater Manchester, has become a familiar face at climate protests. He has been pictured gluing himself to a road and being carried off by police while wearing his clerical collar.

Coleman is a member of Christian Climate Action and was released from prison along with two other vicars on September 22. He explained, "I have calculated that fines, legal costs and travel to court have cost me just over £2000 in the last year. Anything you are able to give to help with the balance would be really kind and much appreciated."

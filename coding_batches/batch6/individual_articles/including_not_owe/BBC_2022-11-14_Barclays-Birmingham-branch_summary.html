Barclays Birmingham branch defaced in climate change protest

A branch of Barclays in Birmingham's High Street has been defaced with fake oil by climate protesters. At about 09:00 GMT on Monday, activists from Extinction Rebellion threw black paint on the bank and spray painted the words "Europe's biggest fossil fuel funder" on the entrance door.

Extinction Rebellion said: "We're here to raise awareness to what Barclays is doing in investing in fossil fuels." The group also wrote on social media: "Cut ties with Fossil Fuels. Your social license is ended."

West Midlands Police reported that four people had been arrested on suspicion of causing criminal damage and remained in police custody. A police spokesperson stated, "We will always respect people's right to lawfully protest, but lawful protest does not stretch to committing criminal offences. We cannot stand by and allow people to cause criminal damage to a building that is likely to result in disruption and repair bills."



In response, Barclays issued a statement saying: "We are determined to play our part in addressing the urgent and complex challenge of climate change." The bank highlighted its commitment to becoming net zero by 2050 and aligning its financing activities with the Paris Agreement goals.

Extinction Rebellion describes itself as "an international movement that uses non-violent civil disobedience in an attempt to halt mass extinction and minimise the risk of social collapse".

The protest action aimed to draw attention to Barclays' role in fossil fuel financing and pressure the bank to change its practices.

Boris Johnson confidence vote: 'They care only about their own interests'

Residents in Nottingham shared their views on Boris Johnson's leadership following his victory in a confidence vote called by Conservative MPs. Among those interviewed were Fiona McFarlane and Monica Pallis, who were in town as part of a long-running Extinction Rebellion protest against Barclays.

Ms McFarlane, 73, who runs her own small business, was heavily critical of the prime minister's actions. She stated, "I think Mr Johnson has made an assault on democracy since he was elected, and by such dishonesty and by dismantling the checks on our democratic system he's a danger to the country." She added, "It's a shame on the Conservative party that they selected him in the first place, and they've allowed him to continue as he has. He's disgraced the role of prime minister."



Ms Pallis, 68, echoed these sentiments, saying, "Like most British people I believe in fairness, and we seem to be governed by people who have no idea what fairness is. They care only about their own interests."

Other residents shared mixed opinions on Johnson's leadership. Peter Gordon, 53, believed it was time for Johnson to be replaced, citing his dishonesty and the negative consequences of Brexit. However, Gareth Bramley, a retired Nottingham resident, felt that Johnson should stay, describing him as having "his heart in the right place" despite coming across as "a bit of a clown."

The article captures the diverse views of Nottingham residents on Boris Johnson's leadership, ranging from strong criticism to cautious support, in the wake of the recent confidence vote.

Comic Rob Beckett was unwittingly mistaken as one of the ringleaders of today's Stop The Oil protests in London - in a desperate bid to get to work.

The funnyman, 36, was running late to a recording of his new Parenting Hell audio book when his taxi was unable to arrive at the studio due to road closures caused by the march.

After fleeing his cab, he set off on foot wearing brightly-coloured Crocs and attempted to glide past the jeering crowds but accidentally got caught up in the protests, sparking photographers and news crews into thinking he was a major Stop The Oil ringleader.

Speaking exclusively to MailOnline, Rob said: 'My taxi was trapped in London traffic due to a protest I knew nothing about.

'So I jumped out and decided to walk so I wasn’t late for my Parenting Hell audio book recording.

'I was wearing my comfy tracksuit and Crocs that are perfect for the dark VO booth.

'I didn’t realise I would accidentally be in the middle of a protest and then end up leading the protest by mistake through Whitehall in my Crocs.

'I had ITV news asking me why I was marching in a protest about oil as paparazzi took photos. I was just trying to get to work.'

The South London-born comic, whose new BBC1 show Unbreakable starts on Thursday night, also gave his 968,000 Instagram followers an account of his brush with the protests.

He had earlier been recording his voiceover for hit E4 series Celebs Go Dating before attempting to cross London for his second audio recording of the day.

Rob’s new Parenting Hell book, based on his popular podcast he co-hosts with fellow comedian Josh Widdecombe, is released next Thursday.

On Sunday, Stop The Oil protesters occupied London bridges and lied down in the middle of the road as they brought the capital to a standstill

Met Police confirmed they made 31 arrests on the second day of a planned month-long campaign of mayhem to protest against soaring energy prices and climate crisis.

The protests were the start of an ‘October uprising’ revealed last week in an undercover investigation by The Mail on Sunday.

Just Stop Oil boasted it had ‘brought central London to a standstill by blocking four key bridges to Westminster – Waterloo, Westminster, Lambeth and Vauxhall.

A JSO spokesman said: 'We will continue in civil resistance until this government takes immediate steps to meet our demand to end the cost-of-living and climate crisis by stopping new oil and gas.

'We need to start a rapid transformation to a fairer society, decided by ordinary people and paid for by those who are profiting from humanity’s destruction.'

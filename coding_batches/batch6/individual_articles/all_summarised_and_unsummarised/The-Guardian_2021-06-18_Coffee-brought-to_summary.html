Michael McCormack, Australia's acting prime minister, has made several controversial statements during his week at the helm. Known for his provocative remarks, McCormack didn't disappoint this time, focusing his ire on environmental activists and inner-city residents.

This comment came in response to discussions about coal's demise by G7 nation leaders, including Joe Biden and Boris Johnson.

He accused the Greens of wanting to "upturn regional communities" and "destroy the social fabric of society." His criticism extended to People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals (PETA) regarding their stance on the ongoing mouse plague in Australia.



In a particularly contentious moment, McCormack called Adam Bandt, the member for Melbourne, "treasonous" and an "absolute traitor" for allegedly writing to foreign ambassadors to deny Australian trade. This comment was immediately withdrawn due to its legal implications.

Throughout his tenure as acting PM, McCormack consistently portrayed a divide between rural and urban Australia, often referring negatively to "inner-city types" and their perceived disconnect from regional issues. His statements reflect a staunch defense of traditional industries and a skepticism towards environmental concerns, maintaining the tone he has become known for in Australian politics.

Extinction Rebellion block London road with giant table as two weeks of protests begin

Protesters with Extinction Rebellion have blocked roads as they began a fortnight of action in London which they say will cause ‘significant disruption’.

The environmental campaigners plan to continue from August 22 until September 5 in the hope of making headlines and getting people to take the climate crisis seriously.

A giant pink table has been placed in the middle of a busy junction in Covent Garden, painted with the slogan ‘come to the table’ urging people to engage with climate policy.

Activists also staged a party game in the street, saying that global leaders were playing ‘musical chairs’ and ‘passing the buck’ over the environment.

Today, activists blocked Trafalgar Square with a marching band to try and force the Government to divest from fossil fuels.

Cockspur Road was blocked with hundreds of protesters in the road next to Nelson’s Column, with a heavy police presence surrounding them.

It comes after an ‘opening ceremony’ was held at the Guildhall yesterday, where three protesters climbed the building, sprayed red paint over the walls and unfurled a banner reading ‘co-liberation freedom together’.

Gail Bradbrook, co-founder of the activist group, opened the ‘Impossible Rebellion’ today with a speech expressing solidarity with nations that are disproportionately affected by climate change.

Demonstrations are scheduled in central locations including St James’ Park, Piccadilly Circus – similar to those carried out in 2019 – but more are due to take place in both north and south London.

The Metropolitan Police said a ‘significant’ operation would be put in place to manage the protests over the busy bank holiday weekend, but acknowledged the activists’ ‘important cause’.

In a tweet today, they said: ‘A group of protesters have blocked the roads around Long Acre junction in #Westminster with a structure. Officers are on scene and specialist teams are being deployed. Road closures are in place.’

They said: ‘We are aware and attending a demonstration along Charing Cross Road in Westminster. Part of the road is currently blocked.’

When XR protesters caused major disruption in London in 2019, more than 1,100 people were arrested.

Major bridges and roads were closed during the action, including Oxford Circus where protesters parked a huge pink boat.

It remains to be seen whether the action two years later will make the same level of impact.

Several celebrities have already backed the cause, with Stephen Fry praising the group in a video shared on Twitter, saying they were attempting to ‘recalibrate, realign, revolutionise politics’ through their ‘mucky’ and ‘disruptive’ demonstrations.

Game of Thrones actor Jerome Flynn attended yesterday evening’s protest at the Guildhall, as part of a crowd of 200 people.

He said: ‘We’ve got used to certain systems that are life destructive, we created them, we’ve become addicted to them, and we know the world is burning as a result.’

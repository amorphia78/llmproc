Extinction Rebellion block London road with giant table as two weeks of protests begin

Protesters with Extinction Rebellion have blocked roads as they began a fortnight of action in London which they say will cause 'significant disruption'. The environmental campaigners plan to continue from August 22 until September 5 in the hope of making headlines and getting people to take the climate crisis seriously.

A giant pink table has been placed in the middle of a busy junction in Covent Garden, painted with the slogan 'come to the table' urging people to engage with climate policy. Activists also staged a party game in the street, saying that global leaders were playing 'musical chairs' and 'passing the buck' over the environment.

Gail Bradbrook, co-founder of the activist group, opened the 'Impossible Rebellion' with a speech expressing solidarity with nations that are disproportionately affected by climate change. Demonstrations are scheduled in central locations including St James' Park and Piccadilly Circus.



The Metropolitan Police said a 'significant' operation would be put in place to manage the protests over the busy bank holiday weekend, but acknowledged the activists' 'important cause'. In a tweet, they said: 'A group of protesters have blocked the roads around Long Acre junction in #Westminster with a structure. Officers are on scene and specialist teams are being deployed. Road closures are in place.'

Several celebrities have backed the cause, with Stephen Fry praising the group in a video shared on Twitter, saying they were attempting to 'recalibrate, realign, revolutionise politics' through their 'mucky' and 'disruptive' demonstrations.

Game of Thrones actor Jerome Flynn attended a protest, saying: 'We've got used to certain systems that are life destructive, we created them, we've become addicted to them, and we know the world is burning as a result.'

Roc Sandford fears for his children Blue and Lazer in HS2 tunnel protest

An island laird has expressed grave concerns for the lives of his son and daughter, who are part of a protest camp tunnel 500 miles away. Roc Sandford, an environmental activist, criticized health and safety chiefs from his home on the Hebridean island of Gometra.

Blue, 18, and Lazer, 20, are among a small band of eco-warriors in a 100ft-long tunnel secretly constructed under Euston Square Gardens in London. The protesters are demonstrating against HS2's high-speed rail development plan and have been in the tunnel since Wednesday.

Safety fears are growing as the tunnel has witnessed five soil collapses while eviction teams work to gain access. Sandford has lodged a complaint with the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) for failing to halt eviction tactics he claims have put the lives of the protesters at risk.



Sandford said, "It is clear that a dangerous situation is rapidly evolving which is within HSE's statutory obligations to address with utmost urgency. This is an emergency. HSE's current response is totally inadequate."

The father alleges that the eviction team has denied activists the right to monitor oxygen levels in the depths of the tunnel and allowed water to flood in. He added, "My son Lazer was injured by a tree root dropped on him from nine feet up. This is putting lives at risk."

A spokeswoman for HS2 said: "We have had police and paramedics on the site and have had the London fire brigade there to assess the operation, and safety of all . . . remains at the forefront of everything we are doing."

The protesters are considering legal action against HSE as the tense situation continues to unfold.

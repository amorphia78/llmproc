Chris Packham hits back at claims of long-haul 'eco-hypocrisy' in clash with Lord Botham

Packham, the popular presenter of BBC’s Springwatch, has become XR’s biggest celebrity cheerleader and is a regular at its protests

Chris Packham, the popular presenter of BBC's Springwatch and a prominent supporter of Extinction Rebellion (XR), has responded to allegations of hypocrisy raised by Lord Botham. The former England cricket captain turned charity campaigner highlighted Packham's support for both XR and long-distance air travel.

Packham has become XR's biggest celebrity cheerleader and is a regular at its protests. In an interview last year, he proclaimed XR "the first real hope of making a bigger difference". However, it has emerged that Packham appears to have been an avid supporter of long distance aviation, promoting exotic and adventurous holidays to all corners of the planet.

Lord Botham, concerned that environmental policies could push millions of people into "green poverty", wrote in The Telegraph: "Chris promotes holidays for the rich that encourage people to go on very long flights to places like Botswana, Peru and the Falkland Islands. Flights which create eye-watering emissions." He added: "Packham is guilty of grotesque eco-hypocrisy".



In response, Packham said Lord Botham's criticism was part of a longstanding grudge over the divisive issue of game shooting. Packham told The Telegraph he had participated in a number of adventure or eco-tourism holidays over the years but resolved not to undertake any more in the future.

XR has regularly carried out direct action protests at airports, disrupting holiday travel plans for thousands of families, as well as business passengers. One of XR's founders Roger Hallam has branded flying a 'no no', and compared it to eating meat.

Packham said he had "not seen an airport, like everyone else" for the past 18 months. He also said he did not fully support XR, stating, "I like aspects of XR but bear in mind it is a decentralised unit, there are things they do which I don't support. They are not a one size fits all outfit."

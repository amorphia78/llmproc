Melbourne: Climate protesters issue chilling threat to cause peak hour chaos EVERY morning and afternoon this week

Climate protesters shut down Melbourne roads. Repeat protests expected every day until Saturday. READ MORE: Extinction Rebellion causes chaos on ritzy Sydney street.

A climate protest organised by Extinction Rebellion has brought traffic to a standstill with promises to continue disrupting commuters all week.

The hard-line environmental movement's Australian faction organised to have about two dozen protesters 'slow march' down King Street in Melbourne's CBD at about 7:15am on Wednesday.

They were seen wearing high-vis vests while holding up a banner that blocked one side of the street, prompting frustrated drivers to lay on the horn.

One of the event's organisers, Brad Homewood, warned the group would be repeating the protests 'every morning this week and the next two afternoons'.

The protests are expected to reach a crescendo on Saturday with about 200 people sitting on the road and blocking traffic until they are arrested by police.

The disruptions are part of Extinction Rebellion's 'December Rebellion' which involves 12 separate protests across Melbourne's CBD, Coburg and Carlton between Wednesday and Saturday.

Protestors are expected to gather on Sydney Road at about 2pm on Wednesday outside the office of federal Labor MP Peter Khalil in Coburg to burn an effigy of a koala.

The locations for where the movement intend to cause traffic mayhem have been kept secret from the public, however are expected to occur numerous times a day.

Commuters trying to get through Melbourne during the afternoon peak hour can expect to be brought to a stop by the movement's 'disco-bedience'.
